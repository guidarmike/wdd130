Colorado River

This is a classic place to visit if you have been rafting before and want a bigger experience. The canyon walls, holes and rapids are massive and nothing short of impressive. The Colorado River also flows through Cataract Canyon in Utah and has some massive Class V rapids to check out. It's interesting to find a white river in the desert, but this is one of the most impressive peaking in June. In its entirety, the Colorado River stretches approximately 226 miles.

Middle Fork of Salmon River

Come to this part of Idaho between late May and mid-June for the best conditions. Here you'll find pretty continuous Class IV rapids that don't give much break in the rafters. You won't find a more remote area of ​​wilderness without traffic. However, you may see some bears around the river. The full course is 105 miles, with elevation gains of 3,000 feet and with 100 rapids.
